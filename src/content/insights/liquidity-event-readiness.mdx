The estate-planning conversation around a liquidity event almost always arrives late. By the time the LOI is on the table, the founder's structure is what it is. The optionality the founder thought they had two years ago has already collapsed into a handful of decisions made under deadline pressure, by the people the founder happens to be working with at that moment.

We work backwards from the transaction. Twenty-four months out is when the structure is still pliable. Twelve months out is when planning becomes engineering. Six months out is when planning becomes triage. Inside ninety days the conversation is, almost without exception, no longer about planning at all.
