When a closely-held operating company fails to make it through a generational handoff, it almost never fails because the structure was wrong. The structure was usually right. What failed was the relationship: between the founder and the successor, between siblings, between the operating side of the family and the non-operating side. The tax work was a hundred percent and the relational work was a thirty.
